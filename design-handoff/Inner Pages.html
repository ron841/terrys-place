About Terry's

Three Generations Strong.

Terry's Place opened in 1980 with a simple promise: cook everything to order, never skimp on portions, and treat every customer like a regular. Forty-five years later, we're still doing it the same way — now run by Terry's son and grandson.

EST. 1980

The Beginning

Terry Built the Bar.

Terry Sr. opened the doors on NE 36th Ave in 1980 with a Frigidaire, a fryer, and a hand-painted sign. He'd worked the line at three Ocala restaurants before deciding to do it his way: half-pound burgers, hand-breaded wings, and never, ever a microwave.

The dining room sat 24. The bar sat 8. The first month, we sold 11 burgers a day. The second month, we sold 80.

"If a guy can't eat it all, you cooked it right."

Today

His Son Runs the Kitchen.

Terry Jr. took over in 2003. Same recipes, same portions, same rule about pre-cooking nothing. He added the wing menu (now 11 sauces), expanded the bar, and put 22 TVs on the walls so nobody misses a Gators game.

His son — Terry III, but everybody calls him "Tre" — handles the front of house and runs the events. Three generations, one room, no shortcuts.
